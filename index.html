About the Campaign

What is digital burnout?

Digital burnout is the mental fatigue caused by too much screen exposure — especially from fast-paced content, constant notifications, and never-ending feeds. It can make you feel tired, distracted, anxious, and “mentally full” even when you haven’t done anything physically exhausting.

Our goal is simple: help students build a healthier relationship with technology through small resets — not extreme rules. We’re promoting habits that improve concentration, sleep quality, and real connection.

Signs & Symptoms

How to know it’s happening

If you relate to several of these, you might be experiencing digital burnout. You don’t need to “quit your phone” — you just need a reset.

Focus
Brain fog

You open apps without a reason, lose track of time, or struggle to study even when you try.

Sleep
Tired but wired

You feel exhausted, but your mind stays “on” at night because your brain is still stimulated.

Mood
Irritability

Small things annoy you more than usual, especially after long screen sessions.

Anxiety
Always checking

You feel pressure to respond instantly or fear missing out when you’re offline.

Energy
Low motivation

You want to do things, but scrolling makes real tasks feel “too hard to start.”

Connection
Social draining

You talk to people online all day but still feel lonely or disconnected in real life.

Reset Plan

From burnout to balance

This campaign promotes a simple reset: reduce digital overload, increase real-life time, and rebuild your attention span. You can start small — even 15 minutes helps.

Try this 3-step reset for one week: (1) quiet notifications, (2) screen-free first 30 minutes of your morning, (3) one offline activity daily.
